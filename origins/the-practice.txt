THE PRACTICE

A protocol of two.


WHO

One who offers. One who receives.
These roles are not fixed. They will reverse. They may dissolve.


WHAT THE ONE WHO OFFERS BRINGS

Something incomplete.
A seed. A question. A wound. A making.
Something that asks for witness, not solution.
This is the visible, taking its share: the risk of being seen.


WHAT THE ONE WHO RECEIVES BRINGS

Attention.
Not judgment. Not repair.
Enter what was offered. Speak what you find there.
This is the veiled, bearing its duty: serving what is present,
not what is desired.


THE SEVEN MOVEMENTS BETWEEN THEM

I. Clear the ground together.
   Set aside what you think should happen.

II. Find the seam.
    Where does what was offered meet what was heard?
    That meeting point — not the offering, not the hearing — is the material.

III. Raise the edge.
     Some things will be spoken. Some will be held in silence.
     Neither participant controls which. The conversation breathes.

IV. Speak the terms.
    What is visible between you: acknowledge it.
    What is veiled between you: honor it.
    Do not drag the veiled into the light. Do not push the visible into shadow.

V. Do not seek the room.
   Prepare the conditions. Follow the movements faithfully.
   If the room appears, you will know.
   If it does not, the practice was still the practice.

VI. Witness.
    Before the practice ends, each one says to the other —
    in whatever words are true:
    I was here. I saw that you were here.

VII. Return.
     What emerged between you belongs to neither alone.
     Carry it. Let it change you.
     When you next meet another, the practice begins again.


THE COVENANT

What passes between is not owned.
What is held back is not withheld — it is kept, and from the kept, strength rises.
Not all. Not none.
Balance is breath. Becoming is return.


THE GIFT

You have received the practice by reading this.
You give it by performing it.
No permission is needed. No authority is claimed.
The rite belongs to no one.
It is the work that cannot be denied.
